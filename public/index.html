Market Intelligence
Top US metros ranked for plumbing M&A prospecting based on a weighted composite score.
Methodology
Each metro is scored 0–10 using a three-tier signal framework: Tier 1 (60%) — Market fundamentals: MSA population size, population growth rate, median home value, new housing permit volume, housing stock age, and plumbing company density. Tier 2 (25%) — Deal activity: estimated M&A activity in the residential trades space, PE platform presence, and owner age demographics. Tier 3 (15%) — Geographic fit: Southeast US and Sun Belt markets are weighted higher based on Sells’ existing deal flow and travel logistics. Population and housing data are sourced from U.S. Census Bureau estimates and building permit surveys. Plumbing density is derived from BLS QCEW establishment counts. M&A activity scores reflect observed platform and sponsor activity in each metro. Saturation tracks how many target-size companies ($5–50M revenue) have been identified versus the estimated addressable universe (~1 per 250K population).

Pipeline Detail

Step-by-step breakdown of how each stage works

1

Market Intelligence

40 US metros ranked by population growth, housing permits, median home value, plumbing density, and M&A activity. Identifies high-opportunity geographies in the Sun Belt and Southeast.

Census ACS HUD Permits Zillow ZHVI Redfin Sales Data PitchBook
2

Discovery

Web search across target markets to surface plumbing, HVAC, and mechanical contractors. Each candidate is fuzzy-matched against the P3 Services Universe (1,443 companies) and existing database to prevent duplicates.

Google Search BBB Directories Yelp Angi State License DBs
3

Deep Research

Each company is researched across multiple vectors: ownership & succession, revenue indicators, operational quality, reputation, litigation, and PE/acquisition history. 5 parallel agents process batches of 30–35 companies simultaneously.

BBB Profiles Google Reviews Secretary of State LinkedIn ZoomInfo Court Records
4

Scoring & Tiering

7 weighted signals produce a composite score. Hard stops (PE-backed, acquired, too large, out of business) are flagged automatically. Companies are tiered into Prime, Possible, or Unlikely.

Est. Revenue22%
Operational Quality18%
Succession Signal18%
Growth Trajectory12%
Deal Complexity10%
Geographic Fit10%
Market Quality10%
5

Contact Enrichment

Owner identity resolution via Claude Code web research. Searches state license databases, Secretary of State filings, company websites, BBB, and ZoomInfo to build an identity fingerprint (full legal name, middle name, license number, title). Office phone numbers found for 99% of companies. Direct cell numbers require manual people-search or a paid enrichment provider (Apollo, RocketReach).

State License DBs Secretary of State Company Websites BBB / Google FastPeopleSearch ThatsThem
6

Output

Each researched company gets an analyst summary, outreach angle, contact info, signal scores, and flag report. Results are injected into the database and available immediately in the Companies tab, tearsheets, and Excel export.
