THIS CANDIDATE IS A COMMERCIAL INSURANCE BROKER. Additional research focus.

SUBTYPE — two very different profiles:
1. P&C / Commercial Lines producer — writes general liability, workers' comp, commercial
   auto, umbrella, property, EPLI for the business itself. Renewals are annual. Sees the
   business's financials, headcount, fleet, claims history.
2. Life insurance / executive benefits specialist — writes buy-sell funding policies,
   key-person life, deferred comp, executive bonus plans, succession-funding products.
   Smaller frequency but deeper involvement in ownership structure.

The ideal candidate has BOTH or specializes in (2) for our purposes. Pure personal-lines
producers (auto/home) are NOT in scope.

INSTITUTION CONTEXT:
- Independent agency (preferred) vs. captive (State Farm, Farmers, Allstate, Liberty
  Mutual exclusive agents — these are usually NOT in scope because they can't broker
  to multiple carriers).
- Large brokerages with producer-owned books: Marsh McLennan Agency, HUB, USI, Alera,
  NFP, Lockton, Acrisure, Brown & Brown, Risk Strategies, Hilb Group. Producers at
  these firms who personally own their books are in scope.
- Agency networks: SIAA, Keystone, Smart Choice (signal of independent producer status).
- Specialty agencies focused on contractors / construction.

CREDENTIALS TO LOOK FOR:
- CIC (Certified Insurance Counselor), CPCU (Chartered Property Casualty Underwriter)
  — P&C side.
- CLU (Chartered Life Underwriter), ChFC, CFP — life / financial planning side.
- AEP (Accredited Estate Planner) — high signal for buy-sell / succession work.
- RHU, ChSNC, AFIS — niche.
- NAIFA (National Association of Insurance and Financial Advisors) membership.
- Society of FSP (Financial Service Professionals) membership.
- AALU (Association for Advanced Life Underwriting) — top of food chain for
  business-owner life planning.

SPECIALTY SIGNALS — confirm:
- For P&C: writes contractors, construction, home services, fleet-heavy businesses.
- For life: writes buy-sell funding, key-person, business-owner planning, deferred comp,
  split-dollar, executive bonus. Has done case studies on ownership transitions.
- Some brokers cross-sell both — these are the strongest targets.

HUNGER SIGNALS specific to brokers:
- Independent producer (eat-what-you-kill).
- Recent CLU/ChFC/AEP designations.
- Active on LinkedIn with content on succession funding, key-person, or commercial risk.
- Recently joined an agency network or independent agency (signals book-building intent).
- Promoted from account manager / CSR to producer in last 1–3 years.

RISK FLAGS:
- Captive agent — usually not in scope, can't write across carriers.
- Some large brokerages restrict outside referral arrangements.
- State insurance department rules on rebating and compensation disclosure (varies).
- If they're at a brokerage owned by a private equity roll-up, they may have non-compete
  / restrictive covenants that limit outside work.

OUTREACH ANGLE GENERATION — bias toward:
- Buy-sell funding refresh when ownership transitions are pending — they get the policy
  business, we get the transaction business.
- Pre-sale insurance clean-up: D&O, rep & warranty, tail coverage on E&O — owners need
  these around a transaction.
- Commercial coverage updates as businesses scale (we hear about it from owners before
  the broker does).
- Sharing aggregate data on insurance gaps we see in due diligence — helps them position.
