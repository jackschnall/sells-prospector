THIS CANDIDATE IS A COMMERCIAL LENDER OR SBA LOAN OFFICER. Additional research focus:

INSTITUTION CONTEXT:
- Bank name, asset size, charter type (community bank, regional, national, credit union).
- Is the institution an SBA Preferred Lender (PLP)? Check SBA's public Preferred Lenders
  list. PLPs can approve SBA loans without SBA review — they move faster, do more deals.
- 7(a) volume — check SBA Lender 100 list. Top SBA lenders by 7(a) volume include Live
  Oak, Newtek, Huntington, Byline, US Bank, Celtic, etc.
- Bank's lending sweet spot — what loan size do they prefer for SBA 7(a) and conventional
  commercial? Smaller community banks often top out around $5M; larger players do $5M+.

ROLE CONTEXT (the actual title matters a lot):
- "SBA Business Development Officer" or "SBA BDO" — these are origination specialists,
  highest-priority targets. They live and die by deal flow.
- "Commercial Relationship Manager" — broader role, manages existing borrowers AND
  originates. Strong target if junior.
- "Commercial Loan Officer" / "Commercial Banker" — origination-focused, usually has
  a portfolio they grow.
- "Credit Analyst" promoted to a lending role recently — junior, hungry, building book.
- AVOID: "Underwriter" (no origination role), "Loan Servicer" (back office), "Branch
  Manager" (retail).

CREDENTIALS / TRAINING:
- NAGGL (National Association of Government Guaranteed Lenders) training / certifications.
- RMA (Risk Management Association) credentials.
- SBA-specific training programs.
- Bank's own commercial credit training graduate.

SPECIALTY SIGNALS — confirm:
- Originates loans in the $500K–$10M range (our ICP zone).
- Does acquisition financing, not just working capital / real estate.
- Familiarity with trades / home services SIC codes — confirm from LinkedIn posts or
  deal announcements they're tagged in.

RISK FLAGS:
- Bank policy on referral compensation to non-employees — many banks restrict.
- Bank has an internal M&A advisory arm or capital markets group.
- Loan officer is at a bank with a national-level M&A referral partnership (some big
  banks have these — confirm).

OUTREACH ANGLE GENERATION — bias toward:
- Acquisition financing referrals: we have buyers in market who need SBA 7(a) or
  conventional acquisition debt. This is direct deal flow for the lender.
- Refinance opportunities from sellers post-close (owners often need refi on personal
  real estate or buyer needs to refi).
- "Aging-out borrowers" play: ask the lender if any of their existing borrowers are
  aging out without a succession plan — we can help those owners exit.
- Sharing aggregate deal data on debt structures in their geo to help them price.
