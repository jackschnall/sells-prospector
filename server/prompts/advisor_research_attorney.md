THIS CANDIDATE IS AN ATTORNEY. Additional research focus:

VERIFY ON STATE BAR DIRECTORY:
- Bar admission date(s), state(s), good standing status.
- Disciplinary history.
- Practice area self-description.

CREDENTIALS / RECOGNITION TO LOOK FOR:
- JD school and graduation year (career-stage signal).
- LL.M. in Taxation (signals tax/estate sophistication).
- Super Lawyers / Rising Stars listing.
- Martindale-Hubbell rating.
- "Best Lawyers" / Chambers ranking.
- State bar committee memberships (M&A, business law, tax, estate planning sections).
- ACTEC fellowship (estate planning gold standard).
- Exit Planning Institute membership.

FIRM CONTEXT (firm size is NOT the disqualifier — career stage is):
- Solo, boutique (under ~15 attorneys), regional mid-size, AmLaw 200, AmLaw 100, AmLaw 50?
- For boutique / small firms: is this practice growing? Hiring?
- For mid-size and larger: where is this person in the partnership track? A senior associate
  or junior partner at AmLaw 200 firms is a strong target IF they have personal book-building
  incentive. A senior partner at AmLaw 100 is NOT — they have established referral lanes.
- BigLaw senior associates often have origination credit programs — confirm if so.

SPECIALTY SIGNALS — confirm individual practice (not just firm marketing):
- M&A — buy-side and/or sell-side experience.
- Business succession planning.
- Estate planning, trusts, gifting strategies (CRT, CRUT, GRAT, IDGT, SLAT).
- ESOP transactions (specialized — high value if present).
- Buy-sell agreements, operating agreements, shareholder agreements.
- For our ICP specifically: experience with closely-held businesses, contractors, trades.

RISK FLAGS — bar rules are stricter than other types:
- ABA Model Rule 7.2(b) and state equivalents on referral fees TO non-lawyers — most
  states allow attorney-to-attorney fee-sharing only. Lawyers generally CANNOT pay a
  referral fee to a non-lawyer (us) for client referrals. They CAN, however, receive
  client referrals from us with no fee involved — pure reciprocal arrangement is fine.
- ABA Model Rule 5.4 prohibits fee-sharing with non-lawyers — IMPORTANT: this means we
  CANNOT structure a referral fee TO an attorney for clients they send us in most states.
- The reciprocal "I send you work, you send me work, no money changes hands" model is
  generally compliant. Flag state-specific deviations.
- Firm has its own M&A advisory arm (rare in boutique, common in BigLaw).
- Existing exclusive referral relationship with another advisory firm.
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure requirements when accepting referrals.

OUTREACH ANGLE GENERATION — bias toward:
- Buy-sell agreement drafting and review for owners we're taking to market.
- Estate planning and gifting structures BEFORE a sale (timing-sensitive — must be pre-LOI).
- Operating agreement clean-up for owners with messy cap tables.
- Joint content on "succession planning for trades businesses."
- DO NOT lead with referral fees — bar rules make this awkward; lead with reciprocal client flow.
