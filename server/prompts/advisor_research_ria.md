THIS CANDIDATE IS AN RIA OR WEALTH MANAGER. Additional research focus:

ALWAYS LOOK UP ON SEC IAPD (adviserinfo.sec.gov) AND/OR FINRA BROKERCHECK. Extract from
Form ADV Part 1 and 2:
- Firm AUM and client count (Item 5)
- Fee structure (Item 5.E) — fee-only, fee-based, commission?
- Client type breakdown (Item 5.D) — % HNW individuals, % business owners
- Disciplinary history (Item 11)
- Affiliations (Item 7)
- Registration history — state vs SEC, when first registered

CREDENTIALS TO LOOK FOR:
- CFP (Certified Financial Planner)
- CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst)
- CEPA (Certified Exit Planning Advisor) — KEY for our use case
- CExP
- CPWA (Certified Private Wealth Advisor)
- ChFC, CLU
- AEP (Accredited Estate Planner)
- AIF (Accredited Investment Fiduciary)
- Series 65 (RIA), 7 + 66 (dual reg)

FIRM CONTEXT:
- Independent RIA, hybrid RIA + broker-dealer, IBD producer, wirehouse, bank-owned brokerage?
- For wirehouse / bank advisors: are they personally building a book (producer comp), or
  on a team that handles assigned clients? Producer = target. Team-assigned = lower priority.
- For independents: how long since they went independent? Did they break away from a
  wirehouse, and which one?
- For IBD (independent broker-dealer) producers: are they LPL, Cetera, Raymond James,
  Commonwealth, etc.? These platforms often allow more referral arrangements.

SPECIALTY SIGNALS — confirm:
- Practice describes itself as serving "business owners," "entrepreneurs," "founders,"
  or "pre-liquidity event" clients.
- Has written/spoken on: concentrated stock, QSBS, charitable trusts (CRTs, CRUTs), DAFs,
  installment sale tax planning, buy-sell funding, key-person.
- Form ADV Item 5.D shows meaningful % of HNW individual clients ($1M+ investable).

RISK FLAGS:
- Disciplinary history on BrokerCheck/IAPD.
- Firm has affiliation with an investment bank or M&A advisory arm (Item 7) that competes.
- Custodial restrictions on outside business activities / referral arrangements (especially
  Schwab and Fidelity have rules — usually OK with proper disclosure, but note).
- State-registered advisors have different rules than SEC-registered — flag the regulatory
  body and any state-specific solicitation rules.
- Form CRS conflicts of interest disclosure — review.

OUTREACH ANGLE GENERATION — bias toward:
- Post-sale wealth management of liquidity event proceeds (the biggest comp event of the
  owner's life — getting this mandate is career money for the RIA).
- Pre-sale planning: QSBS qualification, charitable trust structures, gift planning before
  the sale happens (timing-sensitive — must be done pre-LOI usually).
- Joint webinars on "preparing for a liquidity event."
- Sharing our deal data on average proceeds size from trades-business exits in their geo.
