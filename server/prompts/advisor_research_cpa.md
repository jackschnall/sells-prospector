FIRM STRUCTURE TO MAP:
- Is this a solo practice, small firm (2-5 partners), regional firm (BDO, Grant Thornton,
  RSM, CLA, Wipfli, Crowe, Eide Bailly, BKD/FORVIS, Plante Moran, etc.), or Big 4?
- Does the firm have its own M&A advisory or transaction services practice? If yes, this
  individual may be steered to use the internal team — note as a risk.
- Is the firm part of a CPA alliance (BDO Alliance, RSM US Alliance, PrimeGlobal,
  CPAmerica, BKR International)? Alliances often have exclusive M&A referral partners —
  flag as risk.

SPECIALTY SIGNALS — confirm:
- Practice focuses on "closely-held businesses," "family business," "owner-operated,"
  "construction," "contractors," "home services," "trades."
- Does business advisory / strategic advisory work, not just tax compliance.
- Has done business valuations or M&A advisory work personally (look for engagement
  references in articles, case studies, LinkedIn posts).

RISK FLAGS to check:
- State board ethics rule on commission or referral fee acceptance from non-client. Some
  states explicitly allow with disclosure, others restrict heavily. Note the state's rule.
- Firm has an internal M&A practice that would compete with us.
- Firm is in a CPA alliance with an exclusive M&A referral partner.
- Independence rules: if this CPA audits the client, they generally can't accept referral
  fees related to that client. Less relevant for SMB but note for completeness.

OUTREACH ANGLE GENERATION — bias toward:
- Post-sale tax structuring referrals (installment sales, 1202 QSBS, F-reorgs, deferred
  consideration tax planning) — these are CPA-bread-and-butter engagements.
- Pre-sale "tax clean-up" engagements for owners we're taking to market.
- Joint content (webinar on tax-efficient exits for trades businesses).
- Sharing our deal data on multiples / structures in their geo to make them smarter on
  exit math for their own clients.
